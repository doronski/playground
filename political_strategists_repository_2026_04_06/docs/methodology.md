# Methodology

## What this ranking is
This is a reasoned ranking of the 100 most consequential political strategists in recorded history.

It is **not** a moral ranking.
It is **not** a popularity ranking.
It is **not** a ranking of the best heads of government, generals, or philosophers simpliciter.

I ranked people by a composite of:
1. Strategic originality
2. Real-world consequence
3. Durability of the strategic model they built or theorized
4. Ability to convert ideas into institutions, movements, parties, campaigns, or states
5. Cross-generational influence

## How I interpreted "major works"
For some figures, "major works" are books or treatises.
For others, the major work is a campaign architecture, a constitutional settlement, a party machine, a diplomatic system, or a state-building program.

So "major works" here means the most important **strategic artefacts**, whether textual or institutional.

## Important caveats
- "Best" is inherently contestable. I have treated it as **strategically strongest and most consequential**.
- Several people here were ethically disastrous. Inclusion does not imply approval.
- Ancient and medieval figures often survive through fragmentary textual traditions. In those cases the repository leans more heavily on discovery artefacts than on definitive critical editions.
- For modern strategists, many relevant artefacts remain copyrighted, archival, scattered, or only partially digitized.

## How to use the repository
- `data/top_100_political_strategists.csv` contains the ranked list.
- `data/artefacts_index.csv` contains discovery links and exact public-domain links where available.
- `downloads/` contains a subset of downloaded public-domain or openly accessible texts.
- `sources/` contains the source registry used to build the repository.

## Legal and practical limits
I prioritized:
- public-domain texts
- open archives
- library discovery pages
- digitized manuscript collections
- exact URLs for major primary texts when openly available

I did **not** bulk-download copyrighted commercial books.
Instead, I indexed discoverable legal sources for them.
